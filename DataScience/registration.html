Data Science - 1 day online workshop (registration links at bottom of page)

  
Without a doubt you've heard about the potential of Data Science and Machine Learning.
There are many proven applications of this new technology throughout industries and business functions, and many new cases are still waiting to be discovered.

For the professional there's an enormous opportunity to ride this wave of innovation.

This workshop is aimed at those technical professionals that would like to broaden their skillset with DS/ML:
- Application developers that would like to add ML to their applications or business flows.
- Advanced users of analytics that would like to add predictions to their dashboards.
- Database experts that would like to create ML models for data that's stored in databases.
- Other professionals with technical affinity.

Knowledge prerequisites: The workshop is unique in its intuitive approach that requires little mathematical knowledge. And although it would help if you already know some Python or SQL, these are not absolute requirements.

In the theoretical part of the workshop you will learn about the steps of the data science process, the languages, concepts such as supervised/unsupervised learning, traditional algorithms as well as neural networks, train/test split, et cetera. In the practical part of the workshop you will learn about the various tools that are at your disposal, ranging from open source Python and Scikit-Learn to Oracle's proprietary solutions. You will practice with several hands-on exercises with all of these products. The trainers are available to answer any questions you may have during the exercises.

To do the exercises participants can use their existing Oracle Cloud account, or get access to a free Oracle Cloud trial account. A number of days before the workshop you will receive instructions on how to get access to and configure your Oracle Cloud.

Presented by: Jeroen Kloosterman - Product Strategy Manager, Lyudmil Pelov - A Team Solution Architect, Fredrick Bergstrand - Tech Solution Engineer and Hans Viehmann - Group Manager Spatial and Graph.
